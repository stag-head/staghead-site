§ 01

Meet the team

Northgate Trading Co. is preparing its FY2027 annual budget. Four people are involved in the Sales department process.

PP

Penny Profit

Finance Manager

Penny owns the budgeting process. She sets up Budget Pro, creates the FY2027 cycle, and assigns budget responsibilities to the team. She approves Steve's submission and has full visibility over all assignments.

SS

Steve Strategy

Sales Department Head

Steve is responsible for the Sales department budget. He submits department-level expenses such as administration, travel, and software. He is also the approver for Sam and Rachel's individual revenue assignments.

SS

Sam Sales

Salesperson

Sam handles a portfolio of accounts and is responsible for budgeting their expected sales revenue for FY2027. They export their assigned template to Excel, enter monthly figures, and submit to Steve for approval.

RR

Rachel Revenue

Salesperson

Rachel manages a separate account portfolio and follows the same process as Sam — entering and submitting her revenue budget figures through the Excel round-trip workflow.

§ 02

Penny sets up FY2027

PP

Penny Profit

Finance Manager · Budget Pro Admin

Budget Approval Setup

Before creating any assignments, Penny checks that the Budget Approval Setup is configured correctly. She opens it from her Finance Manager Role Center. The setup was done once when Budget Pro was first installed — she confirms the approval dimension is set to BUDGSTATUS, with unapproved entries stamped DRAFT and approved entries stamped APPROVED.

Creating the FY2027 Budget Cycle

Penny opens Budget Cycles and creates a new cycle for the year:

FieldValue
CodeFY2027
NameFinancial Year 2027
Budget NameFY2027
Submission Start Date01/11/2026
Submission End Date30/11/2026
StatusOpen (changed to Collecting when ready)

Creating Budget Assignments

Penny creates three Budget Assignments — one for each person who will be submitting figures. All three are linked to the FY2027 cycle and the FY2027 G/L Budget, and all are scoped to the SALES department dimension.

CodeOwnerCoversApprover
SALES-SAMSam SalesSales revenue — Sam's accountsSteve Strategy
SALES-RACHELRachel RevenueSales revenue — Rachel's accountsSteve Strategy
SALES-STEVESteve StrategyDept expenses — admin, travel, softwarePenny Profit

Each assignment has a G/L Account Filter set so owners can only import entries for the accounts relevant to their scope — revenue accounts for Sam and Rachel, expense accounts for Steve.

Marking Steve as Budget Administrator

Because Steve needs to see Sam and Rachel's assignments to review and approve them, Penny checks that Steve's User Setup has Budget Administrator ticked. Without this, Steve would only see SALES-STEVE in his list. With it ticked, he sees all three.

Note: Sam and Rachel do not need this flag. They will only ever see their own assignment — SALES-SAM and SALES-RACHEL respectively — which is exactly the right level of visibility for budget owners.

With setup complete, Penny clicks Set Collecting on the FY2027 cycle to open the submission window and notifies the team that budgets are due by 30 November.

§ 03

Sam enters her revenue budget

SS

Sam Sales

Salesperson · Budget Pro User

Sam logs into Business Central and opens Budget Assignments from the Role Center. The list shows only one row — SALES-SAM. There is no way for Sam to see Rachel's figures or Steve's department expenses.

Sam opens the SALES-SAM assignment card and clicks Export to Excel. The downloaded workbook contains:

  • One row per revenue account she is permitted to budget
  • A Prior Year Actuals column pre-populated with last year's figures as a reference baseline
  • Twelve monthly columns for FY2027 (Jan–Dec 2027)
  • A read-only Metadata sheet confirming the assignment details

Sam fills in her monthly sales revenue figures based on her pipeline and the prior-year reference. When done, she uses Import Budget from Excel in BC to import the file. Budget Pro validates the accounts and dates, overrides the dimension values from the file with SALES-SAM's own dimension settings, and stamps every entry with the DRAFT approval dimension value. The assignment status moves from Open to Started.

Sam reviews her imported figures in the Budget Entries view, makes one adjustment directly in BC, then clicks Send for Approval. The status moves to Pending Approval and Steve receives a notification that Sam's budget is waiting for his review.

§ 04

Rachel enters her revenue budget

RR

Rachel Revenue

Salesperson · Budget Pro User

Rachel follows the same process as Sam — she logs in, opens her Budget Assignment, exports to Excel, enters her monthly revenue figures for her account portfolio, and imports the file back into BC.

Rachel's workbook shows a different prior-year actuals baseline reflecting her own accounts. Like Sam's assignment, the dimension values in the Excel file are ignored on import — Budget Pro always stamps the values configured on SALES-RACHEL, so Rachel cannot accidentally post entries to the wrong department or dimension combination.

Rachel submits for approval. Her assignment status moves to Pending Approval and Steve is notified again — both Sam and Rachel's budgets are now waiting for his review.

§ 05

Steve reviews and approves

SS

Steve Strategy

Sales Department Head · Budget Pro Admin

Steve opens his Approver Work Queue from the Role Center. It shows the two assignments pending his review — SALES-SAM and SALES-RACHEL. His own assignment, SALES-STEVE, is not shown here because it is not pending approval.

Approving Sam's budget

Steve opens SALES-SAM and reviews Sam's figures in the Budget Entries view. The numbers look reasonable against the prior-year actuals and Steve's expectations for her accounts. He clicks Approve. Budget Pro immediately:

  1. Stamps every entry with the APPROVED dimension value, replacing DRAFT
  2. Locks the entries against further editing or re-import
  3. Records the approval in the Submission Log with a timestamp
  4. Notifies Sam that her budget has been approved

SALES-SAM status is now Approved.

Returning Rachel's budget for revision

Steve opens SALES-RACHEL. Rachel's Q4 figures look significantly higher than her prior-year actuals with no obvious explanation. Steve clicks Return for Revision. The status moves to Revised and Rachel receives a notification asking her to review her Q4 figures.

Rachel updates her Q4 numbers — she had accidentally entered annual targets rather than quarterly figures — and resubmits. The status moves to Pending Revision. Steve reviews the corrected figures and clicks Approve. SALES-RACHEL is now Approved.

§ 06

Steve submits department expenses

SS

Steve Strategy

Sales Department Head · Budget Owner

With his team's revenue budgets approved, Steve turns to his own assignment — SALES-STEVE — which covers the Sales department's operating expenses.

Steve opens the Budget Entries view directly in BC and enters his department-level costs for FY2027:

CategoryExamples

Travel & EntertainmentClient visits, team travel, entertainment

For his larger line items Steve uses the Spread function to distribute an annual amount equally across twelve monthly entries, rather than entering each month manually.

When he is satisfied with his figures, Steve clicks Send for Approval. The assignment status moves to Pending Approval and Penny receives a notification that the Sales department expense budget is ready for her review.

§ 07

Penny reviews and closes the cycle

PP

Penny Profit

Finance Manager · Budget Pro Admin

Penny opens the Budget Assignment List. Because she is a Budget Administrator she sees all three assignments — she can see the full picture at a glance.

AssignmentOwnerStatus
SALES-SAMSam SalesApproved
SALES-RACHELRachel RevenueApproved
SALES-STEVESteve StrategyPending Approval

Penny opens SALES-STEVE, reviews Steve's expense figures against historical spend and the company's strategic plan for FY2027. She is satisfied and clicks Approve. All three Sales department assignments are now Approved and every entry in the FY2027 G/L Budget for the Sales dimension is locked and stamped APPROVED.

Approved-only reporting

Because the approval state is carried as a dimension value on each G/L Budget Entry, Penny can run the standard BC Budget vs Actuals report or any Account Schedule and simply filter on the APPROVED dimension value to report on committed, approved figures only. Entries that were never submitted — or were submitted but not yet approved — carry the DRAFT value and are automatically excluded from the filtered view.

No custom reports are needed. The approved Sales department budget for FY2027 flows natively into every BC report that supports dimension filtering.

Final step: Penny clicks Set In Review on the FY2027 cycle to signal that the submission window has closed and the Finance team is consolidating figures across all departments. Once all departments are approved she will click Close Cycle to mark FY2027 as complete.

§ 08

What just happened

The entire FY2027 Sales department budget was collected, reviewed, and approved without leaving Business Central — no spreadsheets emailed back and forth, no manual consolidation, no risk of overwritten entries.

  • Sam and Rachel only ever saw their own assignments. They could not view each other's figures or Steve's department expenses.
  • Steve saw all three assignments because Penny flagged him as a Budget Administrator — giving him the consolidated view he needs as department head.
  • Penny had full visibility throughout and a single approval action locked and stamped the final entries.
  • Approved entries are now available in standard BC reporting via a simple dimension filter — no custom reports required.
